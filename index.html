Know Your Rights

Understanding EU Regulation 261/2004 and UK261 - the laws that entitle you to compensation.

EC Regulation 261/2004 (EU261)

This EU regulation establishes common rules on compensation and assistance to passengers in the event of:

  • Denied boarding (overbooking)
  • Flight cancellations
  • Long delays (3+ hours on arrival)

The UK retained this as "UK261" after Brexit, with identical provisions for UK departures and UK airline arrivals.

When Does It Apply?

  • ‚úàÔ∏è Flights departing from ANY EU/UK airport (any airline)
  • ‚úàÔ∏è Flights arriving in EU/UK on EU/UK-based airline
  • ‚úàÔ∏è Does NOT apply to flights from outside EU/UK on non-EU/UK airlines

The 3-Hour Rule

You're entitled to compensation if you arrive at your final destination 3 or more hours late.

"Arrival" means when at least one aircraft door opens AND passengers can disembark - NOT when the plane lands!

The "Extraordinary Circumstances" Exception

Airlines don't have to pay if the delay was caused by circumstances beyond their control that couldn't be avoided. However...

Courts have ruled VERY narrowly on this. Technical faults, staff issues, and "operational problems" do NOT count!

Established that delayed passengers have the same rights as cancelled passengers when delay is 3+ hours.

Technical problems are NOT extraordinary circumstances - airlines must maintain their aircraft.
